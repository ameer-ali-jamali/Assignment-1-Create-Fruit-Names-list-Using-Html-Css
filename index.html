Top 6 Favorite Fruits of Pakistan

Hi friends, today I'll share some of the most popular fruits grown in Pakistan.

Some of the delicious fruits grown in Pakistan include Orange, Mango, Apple, Apricot, Banana, Cherry, Dates, Guava, Peach, Lemon, Lychee, Papaya, Olive, Mulberry, Plums, Pear, Pomegranate, Strawberry, Sweet Lime, and a variety of dry fruits such as Almonds, Pistachios, and Walnuts.

  1. Orange
    • Pakistan is the sixth largest producer of Kinow (mandarin) and oranges in the world, with 2.1 million tons. Pakistan's world mandarin and oranges market share during the year 1997 was 0.9 percent and 3.6 percent in terms of value and volume, respectively. Click here for image
  2. Mango
    • Pakistan produces over 150 varieties of mango, making it an important mango-growing country in the world. According to FAO production yearbook of 2001, Pakistan ranks fifth among mango-growing countries globally. Click here for image
  3. Watermelon
    • Watermelon is an all-time favorite fruit for most Pakistanis, especially because of its sweet and refreshing quality, perfect for combating the heat. Click here for image
  4. Apricots
    • Apricots, with their beautiful orange color and velvety skin, are full of beta-carotene and fiber. They are also a significant cash crop in the Gilgit-Baltistan region, where they are extremely sweet and tasty. Click here for image
  5. Plums
    • Pakistani plums, known as "aloo bukhara" in Urdu, are extremely juicy and tasty. Even the ones without the juice end up leaving a sweet and sour taste. Their chutneys are tangy and have great medicinal benefits. Click here for image
  6. Lychee
    • The Pakistani Lychee industry has immense potential and prospects due to its thick, juicy pulp and amazing taste. It is mostly grown in Southern Punjab during the peak hot, dry weather and is best when consumed fresh. Click here for image
